Arthicha Srisuchinnawong



Biography


Arthicha Srisuchinnawong was born in Chiang Mai, Thailand, in 1997. He received a Bachelor's degree in robotics and automation engineering with first-class honors from the Institute of Field Robotics (FIBO), King Mongkut's University of Technology Thonburi, Thailand, in 2019. He did his research internship with NEUTRON Laboratory, Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (NUAA), China in 2018. Currently, he persues his master degree in robot system (advanced robotics technology) and carries out research at the Embodied-AI & Neurorobotics Laboratory (ENS-LAB), the University of Southern Denmark (SDU), Denmark, and work in colaboration with the Bio-Inspired Robotics & Neurl Engineering Laboratory (BRAIN-LAB), Vidyasirimedhi Institute of Science and Technology (VISTEC), Thailand.

Arthicha's achievements includes receiving Petchra Pra Jom Klao Academic Excellence Scholarship and Thailand and the Institute of Field Robotics Scholarship for New Research Initiatives. In 2018, he discovered a simple preprocessing that allows training neural networks with an easy-to-obtain synthetic dataset. In 2020, he presented the world's first tailless gecko robot with the ability to climb up to 90 degrees and avoid obstacles by moving sideways.
