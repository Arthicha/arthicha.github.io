Arthicha Srisuchinnawong


Arthicha Srisuchinnawong was born in Chiang Mai, Thailand, in 1997. He was an intern at NEUTRON Laboratory, Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (NUAA), China, in 2018. He received a Bachelor's degree in robotics and automation engineering with first-class honors from the Institute of Field Robotics (FIBO), King Mongkut's University of Technology Thonburi, Thailand, in 2019. Currently, he is a research engineer, working on neuro visualization, neural locomotion control of a worm-inspired pipe-climbing robot, and exoskeleton, at BRAIN-LAB, Vidyasirimedhi Institute of Science and Technology (VISTEC), Thailand.

His research interests lie at the intersection of artificial intelligence, robot control, and robotics. The majority of his works focus on designing interpretable artificial neural networks and bio-inspired mechanisms for robots. In the long term, he desires to invent new robots/methods and then introduce them to the community such that the robots used in daily life are as talented as or better than animals and human.

Arthicha's first achievement was being the only robotics student in the batch who received Petchra Pra Jom Klao Academic Excellence Scholarship. Next, in 2018, he discovered a simple preprocessing that allows training neural networks with easy-to-obtain synthetic dataset. In the same year, he was a recipient of Thailand and Institute of Field Robotics Scholarship for New Research Initiatives and started working on neural locomotion control for gecko robots. Two years later, he presented the first tailless gecko robot with the ability to climb up to 90 degrees and avoid obstacles by moving sideways.
