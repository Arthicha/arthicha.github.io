Biography


Arthicha Srisuchinnawong was born in Chiang Mai, Thailand, in 1997. He was an intern at NEUTRON Laboratory, Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (NUAA), China, in 2018. He received a Bachelor's degree in robotics and automation engineering with first-class honors from the Institute of Field Robotics (FIBO), King Mongkut's University of Technology Thonburi, Thailand, in 2019. He was a research engineer, working on neuro visualization, neural locomotion control of a worm-inspired pipe-climbing robot, and exoskeleton at BRAIN-LAB, Vidyasirimedhi Institute of Science and Technology (VISTEC), Thailand. Currently, he persues his master degree in robot system (advanced robotics technology) and carries out research at the Embodied-AI and Neurorobotics Laboratory (ENS-LAB), the University of Southern Denmark (SDU), Denmark.

His research interests lie at the intersection of artificial intelligence, robot control, and robotics. His works focus on interpretable biology-plausible techniques inspired from what animals and us do. In the long term, he desires to invent new methods making the robots used in daily life become as talented as or better than animals and us.

Arthicha's first achievement was being the only robotics student in the batch who received Petchra Pra Jom Klao Academic Excellence Scholarship. In 2018, he discovered a simple preprocessing that allows training neural networks with an easy-to-obtain synthetic dataset. In the same year, he was a recipient of Thailand and Institute of Field Robotics Scholarship for New Research Initiatives and started working on neural locomotion control for gecko robots. Two years later, he presented the world's first tailless gecko robot with the ability to climb up to 90 degrees and avoid obstacles by moving sideways.
